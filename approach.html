The approach

Test, Learn & Grow applies proportionate evaluation across the whole policy cycle — not just at the end.

The approach is most valuable where there is genuine complexity or uncertainty, where several intervention options are plausible, and where you can roll something out in stages rather than all at once. In those conditions, learning your way forward beats committing to a fixed plan.

Four phases

Work moves through four phases. They are a cycle, not a one-way pipeline — what you learn can send you back a step, and that is the point.

PhaseQuestion it answers
ExploreWhat is the problem really, and for whom? What do we already know?
Co-designWhat might help, designed with the people affected and those delivering?
TestDoes it work, at a proportionate scale, and how do we know?
GrowWhat does the evidence support scaling, adapting, or stopping?

Principles

  • Proportionate. Match the effort of evaluation to the scale of the decision and the level of uncertainty.
  • Iterative. Plan to revisit. Each loop should change what you do next.
  • Honest. Negative or surprising results are findings, not embarrassments. Acting on them is the method working.
  • Evidence-led, not evidence-bound. Use the best evidence available without waiting for perfect certainty that never comes.
Test, Learn & Grow draws on established evaluation practice — including the Magenta Book guidance on proportionate, staged evaluation.
