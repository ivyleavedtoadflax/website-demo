How we work

The day-to-day habits that make Test, Learn & Grow work in practice.

Multidisciplinary teams

Bring together the people who understand the problem, the people who deliver the service, and the people who can analyse and evaluate. Mixed teams catch more, earlier — and build shared ownership of what the evidence says.

Start with the problem, not the solution

It is tempting to lead with a tool or a fix. Resist it. Spend real time in the Explore phase understanding who is affected and what is actually going on, so the thing you test is worth testing.

Make assumptions explicit

Write down what you expect to happen and why before you start. A simple theory of change and a pre-mortem ("imagine this failed — why?") surface the assumptions you most need to test.

Work in the open

Share plans, progress and findings as you go — including the messy parts. Working in the open speeds up learning, invites useful challenge, and means others don't repeat your dead ends.

Right-size the evidence

A reversible, low-cost change needs lighter evidence than an expensive, hard-to-undo one. Decide up front what level of confidence a given decision actually requires.

These habits are deliberately general. Each service will adapt them to its own context, constraints and governance.
