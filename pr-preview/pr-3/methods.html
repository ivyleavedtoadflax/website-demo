Methods & tools

A starter kit of methods that support each phase. Pick what fits the decision in front of you — you won't need all of them every time.

Theory of change

Maps the causal chain from what you do (inputs and activities) through to the outcomes and longer-term impact you intend — making the assumptions in between explicit so they can be tested. A good anchor for the Explore and Co-design phases.

Pre-mortem

Before you start, imagine the project has failed and work backwards to explain why. It surfaces risks and shaky assumptions while there is still time to act on them.

Developmental evaluation

For genuinely emerging work where outcomes are uncertain, developmental evaluation runs continuous feedback loops alongside delivery — supporting rapid course correction rather than a single verdict at the end.

Proportionate impact evaluation

When you need to know whether something worked, choose an evaluation design matched to the stakes and to what's feasible — from simple before-and-after measures up to comparison-group designs where the decision justifies it.
